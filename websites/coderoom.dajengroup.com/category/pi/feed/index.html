]]> There is a car parked on the first floor of the City parking garage with a Z on it, in a lovely green logo.

That Z, stands for ZipCar, not Zorro, though it feels like a sword was run against my chest when they FINALLY responded. My account was officially cancelled yesterday with no explanation. Apparently, it had been under review for some time due to, I assume, a rather revealing recording about my personality/afflictions. Anyone that truly knows me, knows how angry I can get, and a company’s bugs resulting in unfair charges, doth not help to calm one down.

I could go into the full story as I am an over sharer, but the story extends to other(s) who may not be comfortable with everything being out there. So I’ll stick to the pertinent facts of this engagement with a company that still needs some work.

Per Wikipedia, Zipcar is an American car-sharing company and a subsidiary of Avis Budget Group. Zipcar provides automobile reservations to its members, billable by the hour or day; members pay a monthly or annual membership fee in addition to car reservations charges. Zipcar was founded in 2000 by Antje Danielson and Robin Chase. Interestingly, they were purchased by Avis Budget Group, on pi day 2013, the same day Nerd Nite was launched in Orlando (come on down this Thursday).

On the morning of the recording I assume resulted in the cancellation, I rented a ZC (ZipCar not ZeroChaos) to get some stuff for a wild adventure away from home.

I had issued a test reservation on the phone earlier with a customer service agent with the clear indication that it was just a TEST transaction that I cancelled. At that time I was in a room so it would not have made sense to start the reservation as I would have lost time.

While at home, I had an interaction that led to me needing an extension. Image my surprise when my request was denied due to me overrunning the reservation’s end time. I called customer service and their answer was that when I attempted the second booking, I ended up rebooking the original. The problem with this theory however, is that all automated notifications indicated a later end time. This is clearly an indication of bugs that missed QA. Due to the annoyance and suggestion of late charges I went into rage mode and mentioned the police, mental illness and protection of life and property that I guess got them scared that their property might be at risk.

So, after weeks (maybe days, who knows) of review, I finally received this,

Now, ZipCar has the right to do what they need to, if it is in their best interest, so no hard feelings. But to keep me in the dark as a customer, that hurt, because I loved you and still do.
So, on this lovely morning, as I spend another night outside my home, (this time, just outside on the back porch) I’d just like to say, Thank you, TEAM, for everything. The lack of testing that lead to the bugs, the lack of communication skills that left me hanging, and the loss of a valuable service.

class Program
{

static List<Tuple<int, string>> Divisor_FizzBuzzTuples = new List<Tuple<int, string>>();

static void Main(string[] args)
{
int rangeStart = 1;
int rangeEnd = 300;
Divisor_FizzBuzzTuples.Add(new Tuple<int,string>(3,"Fizz"));
Divisor_FizzBuzzTuples.Add(new Tuple<int, string>(5, "Buzz"));
Divisor_FizzBuzzTuples.Add(new Tuple<int, string>(7, "Pi"));

Func<int, string> FizzBuzzify = i => GetFizzBuzzFromNumber(i);
var fizzBuzzList = Enumerable.Range(rangeStart, rangeEnd).Select(FizzBuzzify);
Console.Write(String.Join("\n", fizzBuzzList).TrimEnd("\n".ToCharArray()));

}

static string GetFizzBuzzFromNumber(int number)
{
StringBuilder fizzbuzzbuilder = new StringBuilder();

var list = Divisor_FizzBuzzTuples.OrderBy(i => i.Item1).Distinct();
foreach (var tuple in list)
{
if(number % tuple.Item1 == 0)
{
fizzbuzzbuilder.Append(tuple.Item2);
}

}

string fizzbuzzornah = String.IsNullOrEmpty(fizzbuzzbuilder.ToString()) ? number.ToString() : fizzbuzzbuilder.ToString();

return fizzbuzzornah;

}
}
